Merle Behr


I am a mathematician and statistician currently working as postdoctoral research fellow of the German Research Foundation (DFG) at the Department of Statistics, UC Berkeley. My supervisors are Prof. Bin Yu and Prof. Martin Wainwright. Before coming to Berkeley I did my PhD at the University of Goettingen, Germany, under the guidance of Prof. Axel Munk.

In my research, I develop statistically and computationally efficient methods to extract interpretable and reproducible information from data arising, in particular, in biomedical, chemical, and genetics problems. Statistical concepts that are of particular interest to me include change-point detection, quantile regression, blind source separation and matrix factorization with finite alphabet constraints, sub-group detection in tree structures, and approaches based on decision trees.
